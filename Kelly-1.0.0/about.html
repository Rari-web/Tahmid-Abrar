HISTORY OF LINKIN PARK

HOW THEY WERE FORMED

LINKIN PARK was formed in Agoura Hills, California, in 1996 by Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson and Rob Bourdon. The band later added Joe Hahn, Dave Farrell and Chester Bennington. The band's debut album was named "Hybrid Theory" released in 2000. It was a massive success and became one of the most legendary albums of the 21st century.

THEIR MUSIC

They are known for blending rock, nu-metal and hip hop. LINKIN PARK released several hit albums including albums such as "Meteora", "Minutes to Midnight" and "A Thousand Suns". Their songs often focused on emotional struggles, mental health and personal growth.

IN MEMORY OF CHESTER BENNINGTON

Even though there were much bigger bands by the 2010s, LINKIN PARK had held on to their infamy. However, in 2017, the band lost their lead vocalist Chester Bennington. This was a moment that affected people all around the world and it is said that the band was never the same. Since then, LINKIN PARK has honored Chester's memory. In 2024, LINKIN PARK finally added a new vocalist, Emily Armstrong.
